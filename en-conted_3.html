Adult Education

Students 21 and over interested in other adult education classes

The NYC DOE Office of Adult and Continuing Education offers free adult literacy programs, English as a Second Language, and Career and Technical Education classes for free. English as a Second Language and Adult Basic Education students do not need to provide their social security number. Students who register in Career and Technical Education classes that lead to certification may be asked to provide a social security number or other documents to verify their identity for job placement.

The classes offered by the NYC DOE are not your only option for continuing your education. Other organizations offer similar classes for free or for a fee (some of these resources are described below in the “ additional resources ” section). Do not assume that a fee-based class is any better than a free class. There have been cases of for-profit schools that have defrauded their students, charging fees for very poor or non-existent services. Other for-profit schools offer high quality instruction with additional materials, supports and a wide variety of class times. You need to be a careful consumer of these services, whether you pay a fee or not.

When possible, before registering and committing your time (and/or money) to any program, ask to sit in on a class to see what it is like. Consider whether the class requires class participation, group work, extra reading, or homework. Before you enroll you should ask what is expected outside class hours (such as projects or research). Teachers should be certified, experienced, approachable, respectful, and understanding of their students needs. Having this knowledge before you begin can help you successfully complete the program. If you do not like the class, the teacher, center, or any other feature of the program, try another location or a different school/program altogether. You have many options available to you, and not all programs are the same.

To attend a class given by the Office of Adult and Continuing Education, students first have to go to an orientation and take an assessment test at one of several Adult Learning Centers (registration locations) located in Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens and the Bronx (classes themselves are taught at a wider variety of locations). Registration locations and numbers are listed at: here. These locations also offer other services such as case management, student orientation, and goal setting workshops. Locations should be contacted ahead of time to find out when an orientation is being held. If you have trouble reaching your desired Adult Learning Center or encounter other problems, you can call the OACE central office at 718-638-2635 (prompts are in English) or 311.

Some OACE programs (in addition the to high school equivalency preparation classes described above) include:

Adult Basic Education (ABE) Instruction - skills related to adult literacy needs, educational goals, and personal interests. Adults enrolled in ABE classes can advance to high school equivalency preparation and vocational training.

English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) Instruction - basic language skills, as well as the academic and civic skills they need to successfully complete higher education or job training programs.

Career and Technical Training – courses to acquire technical, trade, construction, or entrepreneurial skills (a high school diploma may be required for some of these classes). Classes cover many topics in many general areas such as:
  • Health Trades – Training to become a Licensed Practical Nursing, Certified Nurse Assistant, Phlebotomist, and in Medial Billing and Coding,
  • Information Technology – classes teaching software programs, networking, security, web design
  • Construction
  • General Trades – courses in carpentry, air conditioning and refrigeration, boiler and building management, and culinary arts/food preparation, electrical, plumbing
