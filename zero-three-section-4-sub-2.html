Cognitive development: learning from everyday life, from playing, and while interacting with others

  • During the first three years, the most important thing for children is to give them a stable home where they feel protected, loved and supported. The mood and behavior of those who are with a child everyday have a great impact on small children, since they observe and emulate everything we do, and our attitudes define their small world.

  • To strengthen babies’ self esteem, it is important to respond to their cries and validate their feelings. Babies establish close bonds with those who respond to their needs and cries for help. This connection makes babies feels safe, and this kind of relationship fosters a positive brain development.

  • Being coherent and reliable also strengthens small children’s confidence. Babies get ahead when they follow a routine. Knowing what is going to happen next fosters their emotional, social and cognitive development. Home rules should be clear and consistent to avoid confusing or frustrating the baby.

SUGGESTED ACTIVITIES:

  • Establish a fixed routine, and describe it to your child while doing it (“now we are going to read a story, then we are going top on the pajamas and go to bed”). Avoid drastic or frequent changes in the routine
  • If the child misbehaves and it is necessary to discipline her, do it immediately and consistently, making her see what she did wrong and why the type of behavior is not acceptable. You should avoid humiliating her or getting angry with her for unimportant things.
  • To foster their self-confidence, you can ask small children to help out with simple tasks (folding their own clothes, putting toys away in a box). Acknowledge their successes and encourage her to try more complex tasks.
  • To strengthen social skills, give your small child opportunities to interact with children his age; help him understand his peers’ feelings and learn how to share and play together.
