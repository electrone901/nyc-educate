What can you do if it seems the child is not developing as he or she should?


  • No one understands your children better than you, since you closely observe their temperament, their reactions and their activities and you can determine if they appear unusual. Although you can make general comparisons between your kids and other children their age (according to the development indicators mentioned in the last section), your careful observations of the behavior and rhythm of your children’s activities are more important.

  • If a pediatrician or a specialist determines that your child is behind in his development, it is recommended that you ask as many questions as possible to understand exactly why they have reached that conclusion, and get information about what you can do about it. The doctor should perform a thorough examination in all areas before referring you to any intervention program. The social and family context of the child should also be taken into consideration, in order to determine if changes in the environment are the cause for a slower development, or if the cause is physical or psychological.

A key resource in New York City: Early Intervention Program

  • Through the Early Intervention Program, delays in development are identified in children, from birth to three years of age. Once the child is evaluated and determined to be eligible for the program, a team of professionals works with the family to create a plan of services that satisfies the needs of the child. All the programs are designed to serve the individual needs of every child.

  • In order to help children reach their potential and the development milestones, those professionals generally work with the families at their homes. Through everyday activities, they teach parents how to transform routines and daily activities into learning opportunities. The goal of the program is to help families acquire necessary skills and knowledge so that they can help their child develop their potential.

  • The program is free and is available to all NY families, regardless of race, ethnic origin, income, or immigration status. To apply for the Early Intervention Program, or to request an evaluation for your child, call 311.

  • If after an evaluation it is determined that the child is not eligible for the Early Intervention Program, the family can register to the Early Intervention Development Observation Program, at no cost.
